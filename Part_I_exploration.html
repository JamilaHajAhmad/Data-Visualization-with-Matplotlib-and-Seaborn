Conclusions¶

Summary of Main Findings:

  • The dataset consists of 183,412 rides with 16 features, including trip duration, start and end stations, start and end times, user type, and gender.
  • The main variable of interest, duration_sec, is right-skewed, with most trips having shorter durations and a few trips having very long durations.
  • Customers tend to have longer trip durations compared to subscribers, likely due to different usage patterns (leisure vs. regular commutes).
  • Subscribers are more likely to participate in the bike share for all trips program compared to customers.
  • There is a noticeable difference in the distribution of member_gender between user types, with a higher proportion of male users among subscribers.
  • Male customers tend to have longer trip durations compared to female customers.
  • Subscribers who participate in the bike share for all trips program tend to have shorter trip durations, aligning with the idea of regular commutes.
